Settled

The Diamond Defense is unique because it rotates for most things instead of only when forced
to, this makes it especially effective against more inexperienced offenses, as it would have a
strange appearance and may prove more difficult to predict where slides may come from.

On Pass

The Diamond looks very similar to a man to man defense against passes, the original on ball
player sinks to the crease and the new on ball player jumps his man if he is playing too close
and approaches more slowly if his man is positioned more passively.

Where it's unique is the off ball players, since this is a man down defense passing around means
finding 2 v 1's and 3 v 2's, in order to avoid this, we rotate against ball movement.

When thinking about specific players jobs it helps to think of them in terms of the on ball
defender, this gives us the assignments, of ball, left, right, and back also known as the two, in
our example a pass to the left was made, now the player who used to be on the right is on ball,
the player who used to be back is right, the player who used to be left is back, and the player
who used to be on ball is left.






On Carry

This is where the Diamond can look very strange, to put it simply, the entire defense is designed
around ball location instead of field location. This means that the top and bottom of the defense
are logistically and locationally in perspective to the ball at all times. So if the ball is carried, not
only the on ball defender rotates, but the entire team rotates.
